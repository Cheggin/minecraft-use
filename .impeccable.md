## Design Context

### Users
Minecraft players and developers who want AI agents inside their game world. They're technical enough to use npm and tmux but want the magic of talking to Claude Code through a Minecraft villager. Used during evening gaming/hacking sessions — relaxed, exploratory, creative context.

### Brand Personality
**Whimsical, Alive, Crafted** — The interface should feel like discovering a secret garden inside a video game. Not corporate SaaS, not generic dev tool. This is something handmade and surprising, where pixel art meets natural beauty.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Mediterranean pixel garden — warm stone, lush greens, deep sea blues, terracotta accents. The hero image (Italian garden overlooking the sea in pixel art style) IS the brand.
- **Theme**: Dark mode — used during evening gaming sessions. Warm dark, not cold dark. Tinted toward terracotta/warm stone, not blue-gray.
- **References**: The existing Minecraft title screen aesthetic (pixel fonts, stone buttons) merged with the warmth of the garden image. Studio Ghibli's sense of wonder meets Minecraft's blocky charm.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS landing pages. Cyan-on-dark "AI product" aesthetics. Corporate dashboards. Anything that looks like it was made by an AI tool.
- **Fonts**: Keep Minecrafter (display) and Minecraftia (body) — they ARE the brand. These are non-negotiable.

### Design Principles
1. **The garden is the world** — The Mediterranean garden image is the soul of the design. Let it breathe. Don't cover it with cards and containers.
2. **Warm darkness** — Dark backgrounds tinted with terracotta warmth, not cold gray. The garden glows against the night.
3. **Pixel-perfect craft** — Honor the pixel art heritage. Beveled edges, text shadows, stone textures — but refined, not crude.
4. **Show, don't sell** — Terminal snippets, command examples, architecture diagrams. Let the product speak through its capabilities.
5. **One scroll, one story** — A single continuous journey from "what is this?" to "I need to try this."
